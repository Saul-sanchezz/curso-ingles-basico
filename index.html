Adjetivo: describe al sustantivo

Sustantivo: la cosa en sí

El adjetivo va antes del sustantivo:

  • The black hat
  • A happy dog
  • A great day
  • The old man
  • The Chinese T-shirts

  • I → yo
  • You → tú / ustedes
  • He → él
  • She → ella
  • It → él/ella (cosas)
  • We → nosotros
  • They → ellos

Ejemplos:

  • I go to school → Yo voy a la escuela
  • You are thirsty → Tú tienes sed
  • He calls my brother → Él llama a mi hermano
  • They have dinner → Ellos cenan

Indefinido: a, an

Definido: the

I have a cat. / The dog is big.

my, your, his, her, its, our, your, their

This is my book. / That is their house.

Afirmaciones

I am / You are / He, She, It is / We are / They are

I am a student. / She is happy.
Negaciones

I am not / You aren't / He, She, It isn't / We aren't / They aren't

He isn't tired. / We aren't at home.
Preguntas

Am I...? / Are you...? / Is he, she, it...? / Are we...? / Are they...?

Are you ready? / Is she your friend?

Afirmaciones

I have / You have / He, She, It has / We have / They have

I have a car. / She has a cat.
Negaciones

I do not have / You do not have / He, She, It does not have / We do not have / They do not have

He does not have a bike. / We do not have money.
Preguntas

Do I have...? / Do you have...? / Does he, she, it have...? / Do we have...? / Do they have...?

Do you have a pen? / Does she have a phone?

Regla: En 3ª persona singular (he, she, it) se añade -s/-es al verbo.

I play / He plays, You watch / She watches
Preguntas con Do y Does

Do I/you/we/they ...? / Does he/she/it ...?

Do you like pizza? / Does he work here?
Negaciones con don’t / doesn’t

I/You/We/They + don’t + verbo

He/She/It + doesn’t + verbo

I don’t like coffee. / She doesn’t play football.

What, Where, When, Who, How, Why

What is your name? / Where do you live?
